Who is Saudi Arabia?

Saudi Arabia Saudi Arabia is a country in the Middle East, known as the birthplace of Islam and home to Mecca and Medina, the two holiest cities in the religion. Its diverse landscapes range from vast deserts to modern cities like Riyadh and Jeddah. Founded by King Abdulaziz Al Saud in 1932, the kingdom has undergone remarkable transformation, growing from a collection of tribal regions into a unified nation. Today, Saudi Arabia is a global leader in oil production and continues to play a central role in regional and global affairs.
